Definitions

[!NOTE] What is a requirement? - need of a stakeholder - a property, capability or attribute that a system must fulfill - documented representation of a need, capability, or attribute - satisfies a contract, standard, specification, or other formally specified documents

^99b738

[!NOTE] Who are stakeholders? - any person or an organization that has an influence on the system, or affected by the system

[!NOTE] What is requirements engineering (RE)? - a collaboration and a process with iterations - the requirements are determined with the required level of detail and is documented according to regulations - stakeholders agree

Types of requirements

Functional requirement - result / behavior

Quality requirement - quality characteristic not included in functional

Boundary condition (constraint) - restrict solution space

Core Activities in Requirements Engineering

  1. Determination
  2. Documentation
  3. Checking and Reconciling

Traceability

Traceability is determining the connections between requirements. It lists the changes done to the requirement, the maturity level, and things that need to be improved.

Legal Bindingness

Word Description
Shall Obligations

Must not / May not Negative requirements

Activities of a requirements engineer

  • select and manage stakeholders
  • check whether all boundary conditions are met
  • ensure the traceability of the requirements
  • control the detailing of requirements

Maturity levels of requirements

  • Level 1. requirements are incomplete and examples are sufficient;
  • Level 2. requirements are reliable, resilient, and may be incomplete;
  • Level 3. requirements meet all quality criteria, and are complete and correct.
